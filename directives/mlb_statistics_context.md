# 🏛️ History and Context of Baseball Statistics

This directive provides the historical and analytical framework for understanding baseball metrics, from traditional box scores to modern sabermetrics.

## 📜 Overview
Baseball statistics collect a variety of metrics used to evaluate player and team performance. Because the flow of a baseball game has natural breaks and player performance is individually measurable, the sport lends itself to easy record-keeping and compiling statistics. Stats have been recorded since the game's beginnings in the mid-19th century, with standardisation efforts improving since the National League (NL) founding in 1876 and the American League (AL) in 1903.

## 🛠️ Development
- **Henry Chadwick**: The 19th-century sportswriter who started record-keeping based on cricket, devising predecessors to batting average and runs scored.
- **The PC Revolution**: Advances in technology in the 1980s and 1990s drove the evaluation of players by an ever-more elaborate set of statistics (Sabermetrics).
- **Historical Records**: 
  - *1951*: Hy Turkin published *The Complete Encyclopedia of Baseball*.
  - *1969*: Macmillan Publishing released the first computer-compiled *Baseball Encyclopedia* ("Big Mac").
  - *1988*: *Total Baseball* was released using more sophisticated technology, leading to the discovery of incorrect historical records.

## 🎯 Use and Strategy
- **Traditional Metrics**: Batting average, RBI, and Home Runs (The Triple Crown). For pitchers: Wins, ERA, and Strikeouts.
- **Strategic Implementation**: Managers and scouts use stats to:
  - Develop pitching strategies and defensive positioning.
  - Study opposing pitcher motions.
  - Identify favorable match-ups ("playing the percentages").
- **Modern Valuation (OPS & wOBA)**:
  - **OPS (On-base plus slugging)**: Adds OBP to SLG. 
  - **wOBA (weighted on-base average)**: Corrects for flaws in OPS by shifting weight toward OBP.
- **Pitching Success**:
  - **OOPS (Opponent on-base plus slugging)**: Evaluating a pitcher's actual performance.
  - **Defense-Independent Pitching (dERA/FIP)**: Evaluates a pitcher regardless of the defensive strength behind them.
